The Million Dollar Move

The Million Dollar Move is the foundation of everything we do inside the Guild. It is the moment when a $1 options contract becomes $10. That single transformation multiplies your money 10×.

Before we go deeper, you must understand something important:

We are not buying the stock. We are buying the contract.

A contract is simply a price that moves based on the stock’s movement. The contract price is what we focus on — not the stock price itself.

Real Examples of the Million Dollar Move

These examples show how contract prices multiply. The stock price does not matter here — only the contract price.

Example 1 — Buying 1 Contract at $1

Entry: $1 per contract

Exit: $10 per contract

Result: Your $1 becomes $10

Total Profit: $9

This is the purest form of the Million Dollar Move. One contract. One dollar. One clean 10× multiplication.

Example 2 — Buying 10 Contracts at $1

Entry: 10 contracts × $1 = $10 total

Exit: 10 contracts × $10 = $100 total

Total Profit: $90

Same move. Same concept. You simply scaled the position. The multiplication stays identical — 10×.

Example 3 — Buying 100 Contracts at $1

Entry: 100 contracts × $1 = $100 total

Exit: 100 contracts × $10 = $1,000 total

Total Profit: $900

Same exact move. Same exact multiplication. The only difference is the size of your position.

Why This Matters

The Truth You Must Understand

These examples are purely demonstrations of how the stock market actually works. The only thing that will change in real life is the price of the contract. But the concept never changes.

A $1 contract moving to $10 will always be a 10× move. Whether you buy 1, 10, or 100 contracts — the math stays the same.
