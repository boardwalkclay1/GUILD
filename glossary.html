GOLDEN RULES & TRADING DISCIPLINE

Study the Night Before

Origin: Golden Rules Symbolizes: Preparation before battle
The market is constantly moving. When you study the night before, you see the setups forming that should play out the next day. The market will always do what the market does — your job is to be prepared before the opening bell.

View Golden Rules

Stick to Your Game Plan

Origin: Golden Rules Symbolizes: Commitment to your own logic
You build your plan from your studying the night before — entries, exits, levels, risk. The next day, your job is to follow that plan, not your emotions. Planning is what removes hesitation and panic.

Review Your Plan Ritual

Be Patient

Origin: Golden Rules Symbolizes: Waiting for your moment
The market will move fast and try to make you chase or exit early. Patience keeps you from reacting to noise and lets you wait for your exact setup, your exact level, your exact moment.

Study Patience

Setups Come With Even Numbers

Origin: Golden Rules Symbolizes: Collective attention and reaction zones
Traders love even numbers. When everyone is watching the same even level, reactions become bigger and cleaner. Setups often form around these levels — they are magnets for volume and emotion.

Learn Even Numbers

In The Money

Origin: Golden Rules Symbolizes: Clean, responsive contracts
You want your contracts in the money — or very close to it. In‑the‑money contracts move cleaner, faster, and more predictably with the stock. If a contract is far out of the money and unlikely to reach your strike soon, it is usually not worth taking.

Study the Options Chain

Quantity & Quality

Origin: Golden Rules Symbolizes: Balancing power and risk
More contracts means more money — but only if the contracts are good. Quality contracts with solid strikes, expirations, and volume are your foundation. Quantity amplifies both profit and risk. Your job is to balance both.

Reference: Death Strike — the advanced strike technique that shows you how to size and strike with intention.

Never Marry a Trade

Origin: Golden Rules Symbolizes: Detachment and exit discipline
A trade is not a relationship. A part of your process is exiting — taking profit or cutting loss. You can always reposition the same way you got in. Staying in a trade out of emotion is how accounts die.

Study Discipline

Not Guessing

Origin: Training Hall / Golden Rules Symbolizes: Logic over hope
Never guess. Never gamble. Every trade must be based on logic, structure, and confirmation — not hope. This rule is the foundation of Guild discipline.

Read the Full Rule

Origin: Pattern Modules Symbolizes: The language of price action

Support & Resistance

Origin: Training Hall Symbolizes: Battle lines of price
Support and resistance are the backbone of price movement. These levels show you where the stock is likely to bounce, reverse, or break. They are the lines where buyers and sellers fight.

Look Left
