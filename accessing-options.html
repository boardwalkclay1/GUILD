Accessing Options

Some people already have options trading turned on in their account. Some people don’t. This page shows you what that process usually looks like, how to find the setting, and gives you a simulator so you can practice the flow before you see it in real life.

If You Already See Options

Robinhood

If you already see an “Options” tab or the ability to choose “Options” when you tap a stock:

• Open Robinhood • Tap any stock • Look for a button or tab labeled “Trade Options” or “Options”

If you see that, your options access is already active. This is the screen you’ll use to reach the options chain and contracts.

Chase (J.P. Morgan Investing)

If you use Chase with an investing account and already see options:

• Open the Chase app • Go to your J.P. Morgan Investing or brokerage section • Tap a stock and look for an “Options” or “Options trading” choice

If that’s visible, options are already enabled on that account.

If You Don’t See Options Yet

Finding the Options Setting

If you don’t see options, you usually have to go into your settings or profile and enable it.

On most brokers, the path looks something like:

• Open the app • Go to your Account, Profile, or Settings • Look for something like “Trading Settings”, “Options Trading”, or “Investment Profile” • Tap it and look for a way to request options approval

Turning Options On

When you request options access, the broker will usually ask you a series of questions about:

• Your experience • Your goals • Your risk tolerance • Your income and net worth

These questions are used by the broker to decide what level of options access to give you. Answer honestly and carefully — if your answers don’t match the risk of options trading, they may limit or deny access.
